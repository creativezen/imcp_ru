The story

Misha Saidov started his journey in psychology at a remarkably young age. At just 14, he hadstarted out on a path unlike most teenagers, taking an interest in Neuro-LinguisticProgramming (NLP), the most popular psychological methodology at the time. Under theguidance of Frank Pucelik, one of NLP's founders, Misha's unyielding curiosity andintellectual resolve made him earn his first certification at the age of 17.

This was a milestonethat marked the beginning of his career in coaching.

Misha's pursuit of understanding human behavior and psychology extended beyond NLP,leading him to explore more evidence-based modalities such as Cognitive Behavior Therapy(CBT) and Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT).

He didn't just learn; he dissected thesemethodologies, scrutinizing them with the aim of finding out how effective they were.

Despite his passion for psychotherapy, Misha Saidov doubted the financial viability of beinga coach. So, driven by the sense of responsibility to support his family, he transitioned intothe corporate world, ascending the ranks as a marketing and sales executive. However, thisdid not stifle his passion for psychotherapy; it merely laid the foundation for his eventualentrepreneurial journey.

Even as a high-performing C-level executive, Misha's heart was rooted in transforming livesthrough coaching. At age 30, he already had hundreds of sessions under his belt, even thoughthe financial returns could have been better. Eventually, Misha Saidov’s curiosity about themost effective psychological approach led to him merging various methodologies, birthingthe concept of Metacognitive Programming (MCP). This groundbreaking frameworkcombines practical knowledge, theoretical background, and transferability, aiming to makecoaching more effective by harnessing the commonalities among the different modalities.

This unique perspective laid the foundation for the innovative coaching model that underpinshis Institute of Metacognitive Programming (IMCP) today.

From Misha Saidov to the IMCP

Misha Saidov's decision to establish the Institute of Metacognitive Programming (IMCP) wasspurred by a transformative moment at a conference.

At this conference, he listened to acoach, who made a lot of money from coaching, despite lacking proficiency.

Tis shiftedMisha's belief that coaching could not be lucrative.

Motivated to raise the standard, he aimedto build a coaching business that focused on a new, more effective modality rather thantraditional approaches.

Eventually, he made the bold decision to leave his job when he was at the brink of becominga millionaire to pursue his dream.

Everyone thought he was a fool for it, but he had seen thepain that people endured, and he was moved by a calling to show them the way out of it.

Thisconviction culminated in the inception of his company, laying the foundation for what wouldbecome the Institute of Metacognitive Programming.

Today, IMCP has redefined coaching by providing a comprehensive, evidence-based, andpractically applicable framework for personal transformation and professional growth.

Underthe canopy of IMCP, Misha Saidov has been able to lead over 4000 students to discover abetter life for themselves with the help of other coaches who are all trained and certified inthe MCP modality.

To ensure that the effective approach that is Metacognitive Programmingcontinues after him, Misha Saidov built a modality to ensure that he could replicate hiscoaching skills in others.

Today, the institute has produced over 400 certified world-classcoaches in MCP, ensuring that the knowledge is carried on.
