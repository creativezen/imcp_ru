A modern ontological approach to training

Being a coach or a leader as a way of being cannot be transferred through the means of information to be taught. That is, by learning what leaders and coaches do or who they are, one won’t become a leader or a coach, respectively. Trying to emulate leadership or coaching, at best, leaves one an actor and, at worst, an impostor.

Ontology, the science of being, is not concerned with the mastery of knowing. When applied to coaching and leadership, it focuses on being a coach or a leader rather than having information on how to be one.

The Institute of Metacognitive Programming (IMCP) delivers ontological (concerned with being) rather than epistemological (concerned with knowing) training in coaching and leadership.

We don’t teach you to be a leader; we leave you as a leader.

Leaders and coaches are not taught, they are expressed

There is a difference between knowing and being. While knowing involves learning concepts, being is about experiencing leadership and coaching as one's natural self-expression, especially in unplanned, stressful events that fundamentally alter a person's way of being.

These events, or more precisely, the way one relates to others, oneself, and the world during these events, can awaken a new identity that is stronger, wiser, and more resilient: the identity of the True Self, or The Leader. When an event awakens the identity of The Leader, it is called an Act of Courage, or simply, The Act.

We don’t teach you to be a leader; we leave you as a leader.

What is Metaсognitive Programming?

Metaсognitive Programming (MCP) is a coaching methodology created by Misha Saidov (more about him here) that has been demonstrated to be effective for a range of problems and life dilemmas.

Metacognitive Programming is based on the notion that man's behaviour is always a result of the emotions created by his, and if his actions must change, his thought patterns and emotional responses must first change.

MCP is a tool that helps you identify your original programming and rewire yourself. It combines powerful tools from psychology, biology, neuroscience, psychotherapy, and philosophy, focusing on helping people accept what they cannot change and changing what they should not accept.

MCP combines evidence-based tools of psychological intervention that lead to improvement in functioning and quality of life and are supported by numerous research studies.
